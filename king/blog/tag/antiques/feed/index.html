]]> Over the past decade as buyers moved online with their everyday purchases, it seemed that luxury and high-value purchases would maintain their residence in the physical world. Purchasers of items like sports cars, livestock, and antiques just didn’t seem to have the taste for online transactions that buyers of slacks and home goods had grown to relish.
Contrary to past trends, recently there has been a surge in online marketplaces dedicated to luxury and high-value sales. While these more expensive items may have had limited exposure in the past through sites like ebay, now there are highly engaged, niche communities that live, eat, and breath the minutia of industry verticals like classic cars, antique firearms, livestock, and luxury goods.
For example, sites like Cattle-Exchange.com, CattleRange.com and RanchWorldAds.com have consolidated private treaty livestock sales for those ranchers looking to extend their reach beyond the local auction house. Companies like ClassicCars.com have compiled listings for classic cars from all over the world. For businesses and manufacturing, MachineTools.com has tens of thousands of listings for backhoes, dozers, C&C machines and any other equipment you might need for your growing company. The antique and collectable firearms market has an outlet in GunBroker.com for both modern and antique firearms. And, for the discerning big spender, there’s JamesEdition.com that focuses on luxury homes, sports cars, yachts, private jets and other high-value items.
So, after a slow start, it seems that luxury and high-value items have finally made a move away from the private broker and into the online, person to person marketplaces of the internet. ]]> So you’ve finally made the decision on what your next big purchase is going to be. Now comes the research and, most importantly, digging in to how much you should actually pay.
A leader in value information,WorthPoint.com is an online resource for researching and valuing antiques, art and collectibles. WorthPoint empowers the antique and collectible industry by providing a steady stream of new data and quality information – ultimately helping improve the transparency in pricing and value between sellers and buyers.  
WorthPoint offers both a do-it-yourself (DIY) model as well as access to a knowledge network of professional appraisers with expertise in numerous collecting verticals.  Their do-it-yourself model is a simple solution that gives users access to over 130 million historical prices and images. This data is aggregated from both online marketplaces and from some of the leading auction houses. Setting the right value for an item is the crucial first step in a fair and amicable agreement. Once the price and terms of your transaction are set, we recommend using an online escrow service – like PaySAFE® – to make doubly sure that your dream collectable doesn’t turn into a financial nightmare.
With a collectable and antiques marketplace that is often fraught with reproductions and outright counterfeits, doing your own background work when it comes to pricing and valuation is a must. Whether you use WorthPoint or another trusted professional, be sure you know what you’re getting into before you get too deep.
