]]> BELLEVUE, NE – PaySAFEescrow.com signed a Memorandum of Understanding with the U.S. Commercial Service to increase awareness of the economic benefits of trade in U.S. organizations competing at home and abroad. Joint activities for creating awareness may include seminars, workshops, training sessions and trade shows.
“As a trade professional working directly with exporters, I am so pleased that PaySAFE is joining our Strategic Partnership Program. In working to encourage companies of all sizes to export, we are always looking for resources to help them make international payments as easy as possible.” Says Meredith Bond, Director of the Nebraska U.S. Export Assistance Center “PaySAFE will be a great partner in educating exporters on those options.”
“The safely and security of Midwest companies trading abroad is at the core of PaySAFE’s mission” says Matthew Medlock, PaySAFEescrow.com President. “New markets are opening up for business of all sizes with the expansion of internet marketplaces. Safe mutually beneficial payment options for importers and exporters are key to developing long term successful business relationships and increasing our country’s export volume.”
 
About PaySAFE Escrow, Inc.
PaySAFE Escrow, Inc. provides online escrow services to buyers and sellers of high-value goods an easy-to-use website to create, negotiate and close trades with financial protection and proper documentation via its website PaySAFEescrow.com.  PaySAFE Escrow, Inc. is owned by Ho-Chunk, Inc.
Ho-Chunk, Inc. is headquartered in Winnebago, Nebraska with operations spanning 16 states and 8 foreign countries.  The corporation owns 35 subsidiaries in diverse industries including: construction, information technology, manufacturing, distribution, retail, government contracting, professional services and real estate.
For more information on PaySAFE Escrow, Inc. visit the company website www.PaySAFEescrow.com.
For more information on Ho-Chunk, Inc., visit the company website www.hochunkinc.com.
About U.S. Commercial Service
The U.S. Commercial Service is the trade promotion arm of the U.S. Department of Commerce’s International Trade Administration. U.S. Commercial Service trade professionals in over 100 U.S. cities and in more than 75 countries help U.S. companies get started in exporting or increase sales to new global markets.
For more information on the U.S. Commercial Service visit http://www.trade.gov/cs/.
