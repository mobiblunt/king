24th Annual Falcon Club of America Pacific Regional – October 11-13th

]]> If you have a taste for the finer things you won’t want to miss our must-see car show for the month of August. Intentionally limited to only 3,000 attendees, this show features some of the most expensive and highly sought after collector and modern vehicles on the market.
Monterrey Car Week – August 11-17
For an entire week, a grouping of perfectly manicured golf courses in Monterrey, California are transformed into some of the most pristine automotive exhibition spaces outside of the Scottsdale Auctions. With collector cars groomed from over 30 car clubs across the U.S., this week-long event boasts nearly 50 events including auctions, scenic rally drives through the Monterrey peninsula, and specialized events focused around specific makes and models. Even with a ticket price of $400, this event is sold out year after year with tickets in high demand from collectors and spectators alike.
Visit Monterrey Car Week’s official website to see more information about all the Monterrey car week events, participating car clubs and rally registration information.

]]> During the latter half of July, over 80,000 spectators have been regularly gathering on an annual basis to visit our July featured car show. Taking place over three days with dozens of events and thousands are participants, this family-friendly event has quickly become one of the largest and most widely attended car shows on the eastern seaboard.
Syracuse Nationals
Held at the New York State Fairgrounds, for three days the Syracuse Nationals packs the full 365 acres of fairgrounds with all the vehicles of any car lover’s dreams. Limited to classic cars with a model year prior to 1980 for the bulk of the festival, fans can peruse the 7,500+ participating vehicles at events that include a Back Yard Build-Off, Poker Run, Ol’ Skool Roundup, swap meets and more. And for those with a love of hotrod metal born after 1981, there’s also American Muscle Sunday that showcases some of the more modern additions to the classic car world. Advanced tickets sales are only $5 for adults and $2 for kids, so if you’re in the northeast and love classic cars you’re foolish to pass up this enormous gathering of hot rod metal.

]]> It seems a little hard to believe, but the start of June is right around the corner! Supported by the likes of GEICO, Ford Motor Company, GM Performance and 7-Eleven, our featured June car show has nearly 30 years of tradition wrapped into one event-packed three day weekend. If you love sun, fun, and classic cars, then you need to put the 29th Annual Classic at Pismo Beach on your calendar on your list!
The Classic at Pismo Beach – June 20-22
Held the weekend following Father’s Day, The Classic at Pismo Beach hosts nearly 1,000 show cars and 175,000 spectators – making it one of the largest car show events in California. Open to the public and free for spectators, registrants compete for one of 150 coveted trophy jackets awarded to Gold Class winners. Those Gold Class cars then go on to try and capture the prestigious Platinum Award. In addition to awards, The Classic at Pismo Beach also hosts charity auctions, Poker Runs and car cruises. The charity auction’s donated items like cars, engines, auto accessories and more are auctioned to raise money for the Pismo Beach Police Department and the Folds of Honor Foundation.

]]> Despite the recent snap back into winter temperatures throughout most of the country, spring is slowly working its way back into our lives. If there’s anything you can count on once the weather warms up it would be the rev of muscle cars and motorcycles as owners work out the winter stiffness in gear boxes and transmissions. While we’re all happily dreaming of warmer days, it only seemed appropriate to highlight some of the best and most popular car shows throughout North America coming up in 2014.
Concours d’Elegance of America at St. John’s
Scheduled for July 27th of 2014, this annual event – and others that bare the Concours d’Elegance name – has deep roots running back to 17th century horse-drawn carriage exhibitions of Paris, France. Continuing the tradition of showcasing some of the most impressive modes of transportation ever created, the Concours d’Elegance at St. John’s has become a multi-day event that includes a showcase of collector cars, automotive art exhibition and vintage car auction.
Shades of the Past Rod Run
Held in Pigeon Forge, Tenn., this two day event on September 5th & 6th is an exhibition of pure hot rod muscle. Now in its 32nd year, this event features some of the most impressive hot rod, custom and classic cars on the road. While the majority of the cars at the show are just that – show cars – there are a number of collectors and classic car lovers who bring their road-ready hot rods to Pigeon Forge and can be seen cruising the surrounding highways; especially U.S. Route 441.
Charlotte AutoFair
A North Carolina staple, the Charlotte AutoFair is held twice annually – April 3rd through 6th this year for the Spring Meet and again September 18th through 20th for the Fall Meet. With an anticipated crowd of over 100,000 this is easily one of the largest classic car shows in America. Both events host more than 1,600 classic cars on display, almost all of which are available for purchase.
State Fair of Texas Auto Show
As the saying goes, everything is bigger in Texas. Looped into State Fair of Texas, this annual auto show boasts more than 400,000 square feet of indoor and outdoor exhibition space for 2014. Focused more heavily on modern automobiles the show does highlight classics and customs through their Classic Corral with various clubs and vehicles separated by make and model featured over the month-long state fair.
Iola Old Car Show
Held in Iola, Wisconsin, this year’s 41st annual event will open its doors July 10th through 13th. Featuring several thousand classic vehicles, collectors will have the opportunity to peruse over 300 acres of show space. The theme exhibition for 2014 has yet to be determined, but attendees will be treated to an impressive collection of custom and classic cars over the four day event.
Whether you’re a car collector yourself or simply love the glimmer of chrome in the sunlight, be sure to check with your local car clubs as there are often weekly shows in most areas that feature some amazing vehicles. For a more complete listing, OldCarsWeekly.com has compiled a very thorough listing of car shows by state that’s updated frequently.
